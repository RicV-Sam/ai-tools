How We Review AI Tools

AI Tool Radar is built to help readers make practical decisions, not chase hype cycles. This page explains how tools are selected, updated, and presented across the site.

Updated regularly

Last updated: April 2026

Editorial standards

Coverage is written to be useful, readable, and practical. Pages should clearly explain where a tool fits, who it helps, what its limits are, and what the strongest alternatives might be.

How tools are selected

Tools are chosen based on real user interest, category importance, relevance to recurring workflows, and whether they are commonly part of buying or comparison decisions.

Update frequency

Important pages are reviewed regularly, especially tool pages and comparisons where pricing, plan structure, or positioning can change. Each public page includes a freshness signal so readers can see when it was last reviewed.

Affiliate transparency

If affiliate links are used in the future, they should not change the editorial ranking or verdict on a page. The site should aim to explain fit honestly, including weaknesses, tradeoffs, and when a paid plan is not worth it.

Review criteria

  • Workflow fit for real users
  • Clarity and quality of output
  • Free-plan usefulness and upgrade pressure
  • Ease of getting value quickly
  • How the tool compares with nearby alternatives
