Tool page

Perplexity

Perplexity is a research-first AI tool built around search, sources, and fast answer synthesis. It is especially useful when users care less about chat polish and more about current information, references, and efficient information gathering.

Updated regularly

Last reviewed: April 2026

Overview

Perplexity works best as a research companion rather than a pure conversational assistant. It helps users gather information quickly, compare answers, and move into synthesis with more confidence than a general assistant alone.

Best for

  • Research-heavy workflows and current-information queries
  • Students, marketers, and analysts validating findings quickly
  • Users who prefer sources and search visibility
  • People comparing broad assistants with research-first tools

Strengths

  • Strong fit for fast research and cited answers
  • Useful bridge between search and synthesis
  • Pairs well with broader tools like ChatGPT and Gemini
  • Good option for users who distrust answer-only chat workflows

Weaknesses

  • Not the strongest tool for long-form drafting on its own
  • Some users will still want a broader assistant for ideation
  • Paid feature structure can be less intuitive than simpler subscriptions

Pricing snapshot

Perplexity offers a free plan, a paid Pro plan, Education Pro options, and higher-end Max access. A current official snapshot clearly shows Max at $200/month or $2,000/year, while Pro remains the main mainstream upgrade tier.

Free plan info

The free plan is useful enough to evaluate whether Perplexity's research workflow fits your needs before paying. Many users can get early value without upgrading immediately.

Is it worth it?

Perplexity is worth it when research quality, source visibility, and current information matter more than general-purpose conversation. If you mainly want one broad assistant, compare it directly with ChatGPT first.
